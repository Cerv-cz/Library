About Hollow Knight

Hollow Knight is a classically styled 2D action-adventure game developed and published by Team Cherry, a small independent studio based in Adelaide, Australia. The game was funded through a Kickstarter campaign in 2014, raising over AU$57,000 — significantly surpassing its original goal — and was officially released on February 24, 2017, for Windows, macOS, and Linux, with console ports following in subsequent years.

Set in the hauntingly beautiful ruins of Hallownest, an ancient underground kingdom inhabited by bugs and other arthropod-like creatures, the game follows a small, silent warrior known only as the Knight as they descend into the decaying realm to uncover its forgotten history and face the growing corruption threatening what remains.

Key Features

  • Vast Interconnected World: Over 40 distinct areas to explore across a sprawling underground kingdom, each with unique environments, enemies, and secrets.
  • Precise Combat System: Engage in tight, nail-based melee combat enhanced with magical Spells, unlockable abilities, and a wide range of customizable Charms.
  • Deep Lore & Narrative: A rich storyline delivered through environmental storytelling, NPC dialogue, and collectible Hunter's Journal entries rather than traditional cutscenes.
  • Hand-Drawn Art: Every frame of animation and background is hand-drawn, creating a cohesive dark fairytale aesthetic with remarkable detail.
  • Original Soundtrack: A sweeping orchestral score by Christopher Larkin that dynamically underscores exploration, boss encounters, and quiet moments of discovery.
  • Free Content Updates: Four major free DLC packs were released post-launch, adding new areas, bosses, quests, and the beloved Godmaster content.
  • Optional Difficulty Depths: A rich optional endgame for veterans including the Pantheons of the Abyss, Hall of Gods, and Godhome challenges.

Development & Reception

Team Cherry — consisting of William Pellen, Ari Gibson, and David Kazi — began development of Hollow Knight after a prototype was created during a 72-hour game jam. The full game took approximately two and a half years to complete. Upon release, it was met with widespread critical acclaim and became a benchmark title in the Metroidvania genre, often cited alongside Castlevania: Symphony of the Night as one of the definitive entries in the category.

The game achieved remarkable commercial success for an independent title, selling over 3 million copies within its first two years and accumulating over 90 on Metacritic across multiple platforms. It is frequently regarded as one of the greatest indie games ever made, notable for the extraordinary amount of content offered at a price point far below typical AAA releases.

Did You Know?
  • The entire game was developed by a core team of just three people over roughly two and a half years.
  • Hollow Knight was originally Kickstarted for AU$57,138 — less than the price of a single AAA game's marketing budget.
  • The game's map system must be purchased from a cartographer NPC named Cornifer, whom you track by following humming sounds.
  • All four major DLC expansions — including Hidden Dreams and Godmaster — were released completely free of charge.
  • The sequel, Hollow Knight: Silksong, features Hornet (from this game) as the playable protagonist.

Gameplay Mechanics

Hollow Knight features precise 2D combat, platforming, and exploration in a Metroidvania framework. Players navigate the vast underground world of Hallownest by unlocking movement abilities, finding shortcuts, and purchasing maps from the wandering cartographer Cornifer.

Combat

The primary weapon is the Nail, a sword-like implement used for melee attacks. Soul energy harvested in combat powers both healing and magical Spells. Players can equip Charm Notches with collectible Charms to customize their playstyle significantly.

Exploration

The game world is densely packed with hidden rooms, optional bosses, lore tablets, and NPCs. A dream nail mechanic allows the Knight to enter memories, battle dream bosses, and uncover hidden story elements.

Narrative Overview

The story of Hollow Knight unfolds across the ruined kingdom of Hallownest, once a thriving civilization now gripped by a plague called the Infection — an ancient, malevolent force tied to the Radiance, a moth deity forgotten by the bugs of Hallownest.

The Hollow Knight

The Pale King, ruler of Hallownest, created a vessel — the Hollow Knight — to contain the Infection. However, the vessel was not truly hollow, and the Infection began to leak once more. The player character, another vessel, descends to confront this contained darkness.

Multiple Endings

The game features three distinct endings, ranging from continuing the cycle of containment to confronting the Radiance directly in a final boss battle unlocked through the DLC content, culminating in a sacrifice and dream-world confrontation.

Main Characters

  • The Knight: The silent, shell-like protagonist descended from the Abyss — a Vessel born to be hollow.
  • Hornet: A fierce, nimble warrior and daughter of the Pale King who tests the Knight's resolve and serves as a recurring foil.
  • The Pale King: Hallownest's ruler, whose ambitions and desperate experiments set the story in motion.
  • Quirrel: A wandering scholar whose gentle journey mirrors the Knight's own, culminating in a quiet, poignant farewell.
  • Grimm: Leader of the Grimm Troupe, a mysterious theatrical cult summoned to complete the ritual of the Nightmare Flame.
  • Zote the Mighty: A boastful, self-proclaimed warrior who provides both comic relief and a recurring rescue side quest.

Critical Reception

Hollow Knight was universally acclaimed by critics, earning a Metacritic score of 90 and an OpenCritic "Mighty" rating. Reviewers praised its world design, combat, art direction, and extraordinary value proposition.

Notable Reviews

  • PC Gamer (92/100): "A gorgeous, sprawling, hauntingly atmospheric Metroidvania that excels in almost every dimension."
  • IGN (9.4/10): "One of the best Metroidvania games ever made, with incredible world design and deeply rewarding exploration."
  • Eurogamer (Essential): "An astonishing achievement for a three-person team — a game that feels handcrafted at every turn."
  • GameSpot (9/10): "Hollow Knight earns its place among the greats of the genre with confidence and conviction."
